01 — WELCOME

A Family Farm,
Open to the World

Fifteen acres of tropical fruit — mango, banana, rambutan, dragon fruit. A lake at the edge of the orchard. Four private cottages Ajith built himself, each one set back in the trees.

This isn't a resort. No lobby, no pool, no package tours. Just clean rooms, family cooking, and quiet.

Embilipitiya is not on the tourist trail. That's the point.

02 — YOUR HOST

Meet Aji

Ajith left Embilipitiya when he was young. He worked abroad for six years, saved everything, came back, and in 2006 bought fifteen acres beside this lake. He started with papaya. Then pomegranate. By 2018, this was Sri Lanka's top-ranked commercial fruit farm. He has the certificate.

Then 2020 came. A political crisis disrupted agriculture across the country. Ajith stopped the commercial growing, planted mango and coconut, and turned toward a different dream — one he'd been carrying since his twenties, when he worked the hotels of Hikkaduwa and told himself: one day, my own place.

Four months ago, the cottages opened. His family runs it with him. He cooks when asked. He'll arrange a safari, a kayak, a pickup from the airport — or leave you entirely in peace. He keeps it small on purpose. Four rooms, maybe two more someday. Never a big hotel.

"When guests come, they are happy. So I am also happy."

04 — THE SETTING

A Lake, a Farm,
and Fireflies at Night

The cottages sit at the edge of the lake, each one surrounded by fruit trees. In the morning there's mist on the water. At night there are fireflies.

Udawalawe is 45 minutes away. Elephant country — big herds, open grassland.

Most people come for one night. Most stay longer.
